This paper presents the \emph{Kiwitsche 2-Punkt-Verfahren}, a systematic
procedure for estimating optimal PID control parameters for complex industrial
processes.
The method leverages Bang-Bang (two-point) control to excite the plant into a
reproducible limit-cycle oscillation.
From this oscillation, key process characteristics --- namely the ultimate
period~$\Tu$ and the oscillation amplitude~$\Au$ --- are extracted and used to
derive an equivalent First-Order-Plus-Dead-Time (FOPDT) model.
Modified tuning formulas, rooted in Internal Model Control (IMC) theory, then
translate the FOPDT model into proportional, integral, and derivative
gains~$(\Kp, \Ti, \Td)$.

The procedure requires no \textit{a priori} plant knowledge and is
non-invasive: the Bang-Bang excitation already represents a natural operating
mode for many industrial systems such as cooling and heating plants.
Simulation studies on a first-order-plus-dead-time (FOPDT) plant demonstrate that the
proposed method achieves substantially superior closed-loop performance relative
to classical Ziegler--Nichols tuning, achieving 5.5\% overshoot versus 57.9\% and an eightfold reduction in IAE.
