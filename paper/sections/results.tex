\section{Simulation Results}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Test Plant and Setup}

Validation uses a benchmark FOPDT plant
\begin{equation}
    G(s) = \frac{0.5\,e^{-20s}}{120s+1}
    \quad \bigl[K_0{=}\SI{0.5}{\degreeCelsius\per\percent},\;
                T_0{=}\SI{120}{\second},\;
                L_0{=}\SI{20}{\second}\bigr],
    \label{eq:plant}
\end{equation}
with $U_{\max}=\SI{100}{\percent}$, $U_{\min}=\SI{0}{\percent}$,
hysteresis $d=\SI{0.3}{\degreeCelsius}$, setpoint $r=\SI{20}{\degreeCelsius}$,
and simulation step $\Delta t=\SI{0.5}{\second}$.

\subsection{Bang-Bang Limit Cycle}

\Cref{fig:limit_cycle} shows the stationary limit cycle.
Averaged over five complete cycles:
$\Tu=\SI{82.6}{\second}$, $\Au=\SI{8.29}{\degreeCelsius}$.

\subsection{Identified FOPDT Parameters}

\Cref{tab:ident} compares the KZV estimates to the true plant values.
The gain and time constant are recovered exactly; the dead-time error of
\SI{2.5}{\percent} is due to the discrete time step.

\begin{table}[htbp]

\end{table}

\subsection{Closed-Loop Performance}

\Cref{fig:step_response} compares step responses of three PID configurations:
\textbf{KZV-IMC} (parameters from \eqref{eq:pid_final}),
\textbf{ZN} (classical Ziegler--Nichols on the identified model), and
\textbf{Ideal IMC} (tuned on the exact plant --- upper bound).

\begin{figure}[htbp][t]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.92\linewidth]{figures/step_response_comparison.pdf}
    \caption{Closed-loop step responses for a $\Delta r=\SI{5}{\degreeCelsius}$
             step. Dashed band: $\pm5\%$ settling criterion.}
    \label{fig:step_response}
\end{figure}

\begin{table}[htbp]
    \centering
    \caption{Performance metrics for the $\Delta r=\SI{5}{\degreeCelsius}$ step.}
    \label{tab:perf}
    \begin{tabular}{lSSS}
        \toprule
        Metric & {KZV-IMC} & {ZN} & {Ideal IMC} \\
        \midrule
        Overshoot (\%)                            &  5.5  &  57.9 &  5.4 \\
        Settling time (\si{\second})              & 76    & {---} & 79   \\
        IAE (\si{\degreeCelsius\second})          & 228   & 1834  & 232  \\
        \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
\end{table}

KZV-IMC achieves \SI{5.5}{\percent} overshoot and an IAE within \SI{2}{\percent}
of the ideal IMC result, while ZN gives \SI{57.9}{\percent} overshoot and
an eightfold larger IAE and does not settle within the
\SI{1200}{\second} simulation horizon.

\subsection{Robustness Across Operating Points and Plant Ratios}

\Cref{fig:pid_comparison} demonstrates the KZV's robustness: PID parameters
are re-identified from a Bang-Bang experiment at each combination of setpoint
($r\in\{15,20,25\}$~\textdegree C) and $L/T$ ratio ($0.1$, $1.0$, $10.0$),
and the resulting step responses are shown.
Across all nine scenarios the KZV-IMC controller delivers consistent, low-overshoot tracking.

\begin{figure}[htbp][t]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.96\linewidth]{figures/results_pid_comparison.pdf}
    \caption{KZV-IMC step responses for three setpoints
             ($r\in\{15,20,25\}$~\textdegree C, rows) and three $L/T$ ratios
             (columns). The method delivers consistent closed-loop behaviour
             across all operating conditions.}
    \label{fig:pid_comparison}
\end{figure}
